User Testing Guide - Client Segmentation Functionality

Hi Team,

As we continue to enhance the functionality of our insights portal, the upcoming implementation of client segmentation is a significant step. User testing is essential to ensure that this functionality is intuitive and provides meaningful value to our users.

Please follow the guide below for conducting user testing of the client segmentation functionality.

1. Select Test Participants:
The test should involve a diverse range of participants representative of our solutions' users from various banking subsectors like Retail Banking, Corporate and Investment Banking, Wealth and Asset Management, and others. Ensure a mix of roles including business analysts, data analysts, and executives.

2. Preparation:
Provide a brief introduction of the new client segmentation functionality to the participants. This shouldn't go into detail about how it works, as we want to assess their natural reactions and interactions.3. **Task List**   - **Task 1:** Ask participants to log into the insights portal and locate the new client segmentation feature.
 
  - Task 2: Participants should segment clients based on predefined criteria such as total asset, risk profile, industry, etc.
 
  - Task 3: Ask participants to save a segmented client list and retrieve it at a later time.
 
  - Task 4: Request participants to create a visualization or report based on a segmented client list.
 
  - Task 5: Participants should demonstrate how to modify an existing segmented client list.
 
4. Observations
Record how the participants interact with the client segmentation functionality, noting any struggles, successes, or confusions. Key considerations include: ease of locating the feature, intuitiveness of controls, clarity of instructions, and overall user experience. 5. **Feedback**After completion of tasks, gather feedback through a questionnaire or an interview. Questions might include:   - How would you rate the ease of use of the client segmentation functionality?
 
  - Did you find the instructions provided within the tool helpful?
 
  - What improvements, if any, would you suggest?
 
  - Was the segmented data visualized effectively?
 
  - How valuable would you find this feature in your everyday tasks?
 
6. Reporting
Compile the observations and feedback from all participants into a comprehensive report. The report should identify common themes, areas of struggle, and potential improvements. This will guide further iteration and development of the feature.

Incorporating this user testing plan should ensure that we maintain our human-centered design approach, resulting in a product that delivers meaningful value to our clients in the banking industry.

Warm regards,
Matt
